Project-based Learning for Microcontroller Based System Design through STM32-F411RE devices
J. Caba; F. Rincón; J. Barba; M.J. Abaldea; J.A. De la Torre; J.D. Dondo; J.C. López
Cenference: Design of Circuits and Integrated Systems
Location: Bilbao (Spain)
Date: 20/11/2019 - 22/11/2019
Pages: 1-6
Abstract
This paper proposes the use of the STM32-F411RE development board (based on ARM Cortex-M4 processor) as the platform for the Microcontroller Based System Design course by applying the project-based learning pedagogy. The course syllabus contains the main functional blocks of a microcontroller that are reinforced by solving small challenges, hence, theoretical concepts and hands-on activities are linked. The STM32-F411RE devices include a debugging chip which is one of the main assets to debug programs as well as to analyze and explore them. This work concludes that this student-centered methodology motivates and prevents the abandonment of learners.
